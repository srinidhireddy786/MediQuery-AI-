# Emergency Department Policy and Protocols

**Document ID:** HP-EMG-2026  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Approved By:** Emergency Medicine Division  

## 1. EMTALA Compliance (Emergency Medical Treatment and Labor Act)
In accordance with federal EMTALA regulations, the hospital provides a Medical Screening Exam (MSE) to any individual who comes to the Emergency Department requesting examination or treatment. 
*   **No Refusal:** No individual seeking emergency care will be turned away, delayed, or refused treatment based on their ability to pay, insurance status, race, gender, national origin, or citizenship.
*   **Stabilizing Treatment:** If an emergency medical condition is identified during the MSE, the hospital must provide stabilizing treatment within its capabilities or arrange for a safe transfer to another medical facility if necessary.

## 2. Emergency Triage Categories
Patients presenting to the Emergency Department are evaluated by a triage nurse and categorized using the Emergency Severity Index (ESI) from Level 1 to Level 5:
*   **Level 1 (Resuscitation):** Immediate life-saving intervention required (e.g., cardiac arrest, respiratory failure, severe trauma). Patient is seen immediately.
*   **Level 2 (Emergent):** High-risk situation, confused, lethargic, or in severe pain (e.g., chest pain suspected of myocardial infarction, stroke symptoms, severe breathing difficulty). Patient should be evaluated within 10 minutes.
*   **Level 3 (Urgent):** Stable vital signs but requires multiple resources (e.g., abdominal pain, simple fractures, deep lacerations). Patient should be evaluated within 30 to 60 minutes.
*   **Level 4 (Less Urgent):** Stable vital signs, requires only one resource (e.g., minor lacerations requiring stitches, urinary tract infections).
*   **Level 5 (Non-Urgent):** Stable patient, requires no resources other than physical exam and prescription (e.g., minor cold, medication refill).

## 3. Admission from the Emergency Department
*   If the emergency physician determines that the patient requires inpatient hospitalization, the patient is admitted as an **Emergency Admission**.
*   The admissions desk will be notified immediately to allocate an appropriate bed.
*   Financial counseling and insurance verification will occur after the patient is medically stabilized.
