# Hospital Discharge Policy and Procedures

**Document ID:** HP-DIS-2026  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Approved By:** Patient Discharge Coordination Office  

## 1. Discharge Authorization
A patient can only be discharged from the hospital upon the written order of their attending physician. The discharge order must be entered into the Electronic Health Record (EHR) system before the discharge process can commence.

## 2. Discharge Planning and Check-list
Prior to leaving the facility, the discharge coordinator or attending nurse must complete the following steps:
1.  **Discharge Summary Review:** Review the discharge summary containing final diagnosis, treatment summary, and post-discharge instructions.
2.  **Medication Reconciliation:** Provide a complete list of current medications (both pre-existing and newly prescribed) with dosage instructions, potential side effects, and prescriptions to fill.
3.  **Follow-up Appointments:** Schedule follow-up appointments with primary care physicians or specialists. The appointments must be documented in the patient's discharge paperwork.
4.  **Equipment and Support Services:** Arrange for any required home medical equipment (e.g., oxygen, wheelchairs, walkers) or home health services.

## 3. Physical Discharge Protocol
*   **Discharge Hours:** Standard discharge checkout is between 09:00 AM and 11:00 AM. A late fee or additional room day charge may apply if the patient remains in the room past 01:00 PM without medical justification.
*   **Transportation:** Patients must have pre-arranged, safe transportation. Under no circumstances will a patient discharged from a major surgery, sedation, or psychiatric care be allowed to drive themselves home or leave unaccompanied.
*   **Escort Service:** A hospital staff member or volunteer must escort the patient via wheelchair to the designated pickup area at the hospital entrance.
