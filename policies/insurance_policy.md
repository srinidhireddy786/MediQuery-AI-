# Hospital Insurance Verification and Prior Authorization Policy

**Document ID:** HP-INS-2026  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Approved By:** Managed Care and Insurance Operations  

## 1. Covered Insurance Providers
The hospital participates in and accepts coverage from most major national and regional insurance providers, including:
*   Medicare and Medicaid (state-specific programs)
*   Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS)
*   Aetna
*   Cigna
*   UnitedHealthcare
*   Humana

## 2. Insurance Verification Process
*   **Verification Timeline:** For all scheduled, elective admissions and surgeries, insurance coverage and benefits must be verified at least 5 business days prior to the procedure date.
*   **Verification Details:** The verification team will confirm active coverage status, deductible amounts, co-insurance percentages, and out-of-pocket maximums.

## 3. Prior Authorization (Pre-Authorization) Requirements
Many insurance plans require prior authorization for specific procedures, admissions, and high-cost medications.
*   **Prior Authorization Obligation:** Prior authorization is required for all elective inpatient admissions, major surgeries (including orthopedics, cardiology, and oncology surgeries), and advanced diagnostic imaging (MRI, CT scans, PET scans).
*   **Timeline:** Prior authorization requests must be submitted to the insurance provider at least 7 to 10 business days before the scheduled procedure.
*   **Emergency Exception:** Prior authorization is NOT required for emergency, life-saving procedures or immediate admissions through the Emergency Department. However, the insurance provider must be notified of the emergency admission within 24 to 48 hours of check-in.
*   **Consequences of Non-Authorization:** If a scheduled procedure requiring prior authorization is performed without approval, the insurance provider may deny the claim. In such cases, the patient may be held financially responsible for the entire billing amount.
