# Hospital Admission Policy and Guidelines

**Document ID:** HP-ADM-2026  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Approved By:** Medical Operations Committee  

## 1. General Admission Protocol
All patients admitted to the hospital must go through the Central Admissions Department. Patients are classified into three primary admission types:
*   **Emergency Admissions:** Patients requiring immediate medical attention due to life-threatening or acute conditions. Emergency admissions do not require pre-registration.
*   **Elective Admissions:** Scheduled admissions for planned procedures, surgeries, or therapies. These must be scheduled at least 72 hours in advance.
*   **Urgent Admissions:** Admissions required within 24 to 48 hours for conditions that are not immediately life-threatening but could deteriorate without intervention.

## 2. Pre-Admission Requirements
For all elective and scheduled admissions, patients must provide:
1.  A valid physician's admission order.
2.  A valid government-issued photo identification.
3.  Active health insurance card or proof of financial responsibility.
4.  Completed pre-admission questionnaire containing medical history and allergies.

## 3. Patient Check-In and Room Assignment
*   **Check-In Hours:** Central Admissions is open 24/7 for emergency admissions. Elective admissions should check in between 06:00 AM and 02:00 PM on the day of the scheduled procedure.
*   **Room Allocation:** Rooms are allocated based on medical necessity first, followed by room availability. Single-occupancy rooms are prioritized for infectious disease patients, intensive care, and patients with specific insurance packages. Standard admissions are placed in double-occupancy rooms.
*   **Identification:** Upon admission, all patients receive a barcode-encoded identification wristband. A red wristband indicates a documented allergy, and a yellow wristband indicates a high fall risk.
